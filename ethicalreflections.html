Kiran's Reflection

I personally believe that VR is a promising new way of approaching healthcare. The benefits it can and could offer, I think, seem to outweigh the seemingly very minor downsides. Providing doctors a way to train for highly technical and difficult surgeries for example without endangering any lives seems very promising. For the patient perspective, I think that VR has high usefulness in therapy for trauma victims. I think children may benefit the most from VR in healthcare because it can make things more exciting and less scary when it's presented in a fun way. I think ethically VR has a place in healthcare, as long as it is carried out in a moral way, with sensitive applications for those who may need it.

Pettric's Reflection

Technological advancements in human history has been a double edged sword. in recent centuries advancements to combat climate change have been significant but so has the advancements in military technological warfare. Our website showcases the advancements of technology in directions that are beneficial and not destructive. The more we look into how technology can be used, the opportunities, choices and the risks posed by uses of technology, the more we begin to direct the advancments of technology into areas that progress human society.
