Another case where we can source usage of choosing to implement learning methods of virtual reality into the classroom is in wording such as “Looking at the world, we absorb the equivalent of a billion bits of information per second, as much as the text in 1,000 copies of a magazine. But our mental 'text computer' is limited by the fact that we can read only about 1 00 bites - or characters - per second”(1992, Helsel, p.g 6). These examples of the influx in usage of choosing to use virtual reality in the education industry shows us that it could benefit parties such as the medical industry in their education methods for training newly recruited prospects in the field and therefore aid health and wellbeing of society.
